## Why this list

When you’re shopping for baby products, words like “eco-friendly” and “non-toxic” can sound reassuring without being very specific. This guide is here for the tired, slightly overwhelmed version of you who just wants safer basics that hold up in real life. We focus on the items that touch baby the most (clothing, feeding, skincare, cleaning), and we favor products with clearer materials/ingredients and fewer unnecessary add-ons. It’s not about perfection. It’s about a little more peace of mind in your daily routine.

## How we chose

New parents don’t need more options. They need fewer regrets. - **High-contact first:** we start with items that touch baby’s skin and mouth most often. - **Clear materials/ingredients:** we prefer products that are specific (not just “natural”). - **Fragrance-free when possible:** it’s often the simplest choice for sensitive skin. - **Real-life usability:** if it’s annoying to clean at 2am, it won’t survive your routine. - **Value over hype:** basics that hold up beat trendy extras every time. *Note: These recommendations are based on product details, broad review patterns, and common buyer criteria (not hands-on testing).*

## Buyer’s guide

If you can only make a few swaps, start with the things you use every day. - **What will this be used for?** - **What constraints matter most?** If a label feels vague, look for specifics: listed ingredients, clear material details, and fewer added fragrances.
